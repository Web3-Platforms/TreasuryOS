# TreasuryOS Investor Demo Script

## Purpose

This script is designed for an investor-facing product demo of TreasuryOS.

It is written to help you present the platform as:

- a compliance-first treasury operating system
- a bridge between institutional operations and on-chain controls
- a platform with real product workflows, not just a prototype UI

## Recommended demo length

- Short version: 4-5 minutes
- Full investor version: 7-9 minutes

## Core investor message

TreasuryOS helps regulated institutions manage onboarding, wallet governance, transaction review, auditability, and reporting in one operational layer, while progressively connecting those approvals to on-chain enforcement.

## Important positioning

Use these claims confidently:

- TreasuryOS has a live dashboard and API with authenticated, role-based workflows
- the platform supports entity onboarding, wallet approval, transaction case review, audit events, and report generation
- TreasuryOS has completed a real Solana testnet whitelist canary
- the product is being built for strict compliance environments such as EU MiCA-style operations

Avoid overstating these points unless separately verified in the meeting:

- production customer volume
- signed revenue
- live mainnet settlement
- fully launched AI automation

Instead, position those as roadmap or commercialization milestones if needed.

## Demo structure

## 1. Opening hook

### Time

0:00 - 0:30

### On screen

- TreasuryOS landing page or dashboard home

### Script

"TreasuryOS is building the operating layer for regulated digital-asset treasury teams. Instead of stitching together spreadsheets, manual reviews, wallet allowlists, compliance notes, and reporting in disconnected tools, TreasuryOS brings those workflows into one system with clear controls, auditability, and a path to on-chain enforcement."

---

## 2. Problem statement

### Time

0:30 - 1:10

### On screen

- Stay on home page, or use one slide if you prefer

### Script

"Today, institutional crypto operations still suffer from a fragmented stack. Compliance teams review entities in one place, treasury teams approve wallets somewhere else, transaction reviews are manual, and audit evidence is scattered across chats and files. That creates operational drag, weak controls, and poor visibility exactly where regulators and enterprise clients expect the highest standard."

"TreasuryOS is designed to solve that by turning compliance and treasury operations into a structured workflow system that can also connect directly to blockchain controls."

---

## 3. Product overview

### Time

1:10 - 1:50

### On screen

- Dashboard navigation
- Show `Entities`, `Wallets`, `Transactions`, and `Reports`

### Script

"The platform is organized around the real operating flow. First, institutions and counterparties are onboarded. Then wallets are reviewed and approved. Transactions that need attention move into a review queue. Every action is logged. And from there, the platform can generate operational reporting and eventually add AI-assisted review support without removing the human approval layer."

"So this is not just a monitoring dashboard. It is an operating system for treasury governance."

---

## 4. Entity onboarding and approval

### Time

1:50 - 2:40

### On screen

- Open `/entities`
- Open an entity detail page
- Point out status, KYC section, notes, and approval actions

### Script

"Here, compliance officers manage legal entities going through review. Each entity has a structured lifecycle, status controls, KYC context, jurisdiction, risk level, and internal notes. The point is to move from ad hoc approval decisions to a traceable operational workflow."

"For an investor, what matters here is that TreasuryOS is being built around process integrity. Status changes are explicit, role-based, and reviewable. That becomes very important once institutions need to prove who approved what, when, and under what operating policy."

---

## 5. Wallet governance and on-chain enforcement

### Time

2:40 - 3:50

### On screen

- Open `/wallets`
- Open a wallet detail page
- Show status, sync state, whitelist entry, and transaction signature if available

### Script

"This is where TreasuryOS becomes more than an internal workflow tool. Wallet approval is not just a checkbox in the database. TreasuryOS can connect approval decisions to an on-chain whitelist flow."

"We recently completed a real Solana testnet canary. That means an approved wallet moved through the platform workflow and produced a real testnet transaction and a real whitelist entry on-chain."

"That matters because it shows the architecture can bridge institutional approval workflows to blockchain enforcement, step by step, in a controlled way."

### Optional credibility line

"In other words: we are not only designing governance screens, we have already proven the first live control path on testnet."

---

## 6. Transaction case queue

### Time

3:50 - 4:50

### On screen

- Open `/transactions`
- Show the screening form
- If available, show one case in the queue and open its detail page

### Script

"TreasuryOS also supports transaction screening and case management. When a screened transaction triggers risk rules or is forced into manual review, it appears in the transaction case queue for compliance action."

"This is important because institutions do not just need onboarding controls. They need operational review after onboarding as well. TreasuryOS is designed to give compliance teams a dedicated queue for flagged activity, with explicit review, approval, rejection, or escalation paths."

"From an investor perspective, this expands the product from onboarding software into an ongoing operational control layer."

### Clarifying line if queue is empty

"If the queue is empty during the demo, that simply means no current screening case has triggered review. Cleared transactions do not appear here by design."

---

## 7. Reports and auditability

### Time

4:50 - 5:40

### On screen

- Open `/reports`
- Generate a monthly report if appropriate
- Mention downloads and audit trail

### Script

"A major part of the value proposition is auditability. TreasuryOS generates operational reports and keeps an evidence trail around approvals, reviews, and system actions."

"That matters for both regulators and enterprise buyers. They want proof of control, not just a promise of control. Reporting and audit history are how this becomes usable in a real governance environment."

---

## 8. AI strategy

### Time

5:40 - 6:20

### On screen

- Stay on a workflow page or use a single roadmap slide

### Script

"We also see a strong AI opportunity, but in a controlled way. Our view is that AI should act as an advisory layer, not an autonomous signer or approval engine."

"That means AI can summarize case context, explain why a wallet or transaction looks risky, draft report narratives, and help operators move faster. But the actual approval and control path remains governed, role-based, and auditable."

"That is the right architecture for enterprise and regulated adoption."

---

## 9. Close and investor takeaway

### Time

6:20 - 7:10

### On screen

- Return to home/dashboard overview

### Script

"TreasuryOS sits at the intersection of compliance software, treasury operations, and on-chain control infrastructure. Our belief is that as digital-asset operations mature, institutions will need a system that does three things well: structure internal decision-making, preserve a defensible audit trail, and connect those decisions to enforceable wallet and transaction controls."

"What we are demonstrating today is early proof of that platform: real workflows, real governance surfaces, and a real testnet control path. The next step is scaling from validated infrastructure into wider institutional deployment and stronger automation around review, reporting, and advisory intelligence."

## Demo preparation checklist

Before recording or presenting, make sure you have:

- one approved entity ready
- one approved or synced wallet visible
- one wallet with a visible whitelist entry and testnet transaction signature
- one transaction case available, if you want to show the review flow
- one generated report available for download
- any customer, pipeline, or fundraising metrics prepared separately if you plan to mention them

## Presenter notes

- Speak like an operator building infrastructure, not like a hype pitch
- Emphasize control, auditability, and workflow integrity
- Use the testnet canary as proof of execution quality
- Position AI as assistive and enterprise-safe
- If investors ask about timing, answer with milestones, not vague claims
