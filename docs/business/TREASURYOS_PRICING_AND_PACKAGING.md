# TreasuryOS Pricing and Packaging

## Important note

This document is an **internal working commercial model**, not public pricing.
It is designed to help TreasuryOS close the first revenue-generating customers
without overstating product maturity.

## Pricing philosophy

TreasuryOS should be priced as **enterprise workflow infrastructure**, not as a
cheap per-transaction utility.

Why:

- the value is in control, governance, auditability, and operational compression
- onboarding and trust-building matter
- the buyer is institution-like
- the first deals should be high-conviction, founder-led, and high-touch

## Recommended offer ladder

### 1. Design Partner Pilot

**Best use:** first 1-5 customers

| Component | Internal working range |
| --- | --- |
| Setup / onboarding fee | EUR 7,500-20,000 |
| Monthly pilot fee | EUR 2,500-6,000 |
| Pilot length | 8-12 weeks |

**Includes**

- controlled pilot environment
- workflow configuration for the first use case
- entity, wallet, and case-review setup
- weekly working sessions
- reporting and feedback loop

**Why this should exist**

- creates revenue before full production readiness
- turns early customers into product-shaping design partners
- supports founder-led onboarding and trust building

### 2. Core Platform Subscription

**Best use:** convert successful pilots to software revenue

| Component | Internal working range |
| --- | --- |
| Annual contract value | EUR 36,000-96,000 / year |
| Monthly equivalent | EUR 3,000-8,000 / month |

**Positioning**

- core institutional workflow platform
- dashboard + API operating layer
- governance-aware treasury controls
- case handling, audit logging, reporting baseline

**Good fit for**

- teams with recurring use
- teams that want the software but do not yet need heavy integration work

### 3. Institutional / Enterprise Package

**Best use:** larger or more complex institutional buyers

| Component | Internal working range |
| --- | --- |
| Annual contract value | EUR 96,000-240,000+ / year |

**Positioning**

- broader workflow coverage
- more operating complexity
- more onboarding and integration depth
- premium support and roadmap collaboration

**Use when**

- the customer has multiple stakeholder groups
- the buyer needs deeper reporting, governance, or integration support
- there is a real expansion path worth pricing for

## Packaging principles

1. **Do not start with self-serve pricing**
2. **Lead with paid pilot value**
3. **Convert to annual contracts, not month-to-month dependence**
4. **Keep pricing tied to operational value and complexity**
5. **Use services to accelerate cash flow early**

## Simple pricing rule set

### Price higher when

- the customer has more operational complexity
- more teams or reviewers are involved
- integration depth is higher
- the rollout carries more compliance/reporting pressure

### Price lower when

- the deal is truly a narrow design-partner pilot
- the buyer is helping shape the roadmap
- the scope is kept deliberately small

## Recommended near-term commercial target

The best early target is to close:

- **1-2 paid design partner pilots**
- then convert at least **1 pilot** into an annual subscription

That is a stronger proof of business than publishing theoretical public pricing.
