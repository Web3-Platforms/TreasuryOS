# TreasuryOS Business Model

## Recommended core business model

TreasuryOS should operate as a **B2B enterprise software platform** for
institutional digital-asset treasury and compliance, with a **pilot-first land
motion** and **subscription-plus-services expansion model**.

## The business in one sentence

TreasuryOS sells the control plane institutions need to operate digital-asset
treasury workflows with governance, auditability, and compliance discipline.

## Recommended model stack

### Primary model now

**Paid pilot -> annual software subscription -> add-ons and integrations**

This is the best fit for the current product posture because:

- the platform is real and demoable
- buyers will still need trust-building and workflow design
- integrations and assurance maturity are not yet fully complete
- enterprise buyers usually need a scoped first engagement before a bigger
  software commitment

### Secondary models

1. **Implementation and onboarding services**  
   Use this to accelerate revenue early while the customer base is small.
2. **Premium modules**  
   AI advisory, reporting depth, integration packs, and governance operations
   can become expansion levers.
3. **API / embedded / white-label path**  
   This is not the first revenue motion, but it can become meaningful later for
   ecosystem partners and service providers.

## Ideal customer profile

### Primary ICP

The best first customers are organizations that:

- already understand digital assets or are actively entering them
- care about approvals, auditability, and policy controls
- have enough internal complexity that spreadsheets and wallet tools are not enough
- can buy through founder-led enterprise sales rather than self-serve motion

### Best-fit customer types now

1. **Digital-asset treasury teams**  
   crypto-native operators becoming more institutional in controls
2. **Funds, market participants, and treasury vehicles**  
   firms that need approval and review layers around assets and wallets
3. **Token issuers / stablecoin or treasury infrastructure operators**  
   teams that need governance and compliance posture, not just blockchain access
4. **Fintech or tokenized-finance teams**  
   organizations bridging traditional controls with on-chain workflows

### Customers to avoid first

- pure retail or self-serve audiences
- very small teams with no compliance or governance complexity
- customers demanding immediate full production KYC, banking, and mainnet depth
  before pilot validation

## Revenue streams

| Revenue stream | Near-term fit | Why it works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Paid pilot / design partner | Very strong | Matches current trust-building stage and founder-led sales motion |
| Annual platform subscription | Strong | Natural conversion path after pilot success |
| Implementation / onboarding services | Strong | Early customers need help mapping controls and workflows |
| AI advisory add-on | Medium now, strong later | Good premium upsell once the base platform is adopted |
| Reporting / compliance modules | Medium | Valuable expansion lever for regulated workflows |
| Integration packages | Medium | Good for banking, KYC, or internal enterprise system work |
| API / white-label licensing | Later-stage | Better after repeatable product-market fit and trust proof |

## Why this model is economically attractive

TreasuryOS should target **high-ACV, low-logo-count enterprise software**
rather than mass self-serve.

Why:

- the problem is operationally complex
- buyers are institution-like, not consumer-like
- trust, onboarding, and integration depth matter more than simple seat count
- the variable cost of software is low relative to the value of control,
  auditability, and workflow compression

## Recommended land-and-expand motion

### Land

Sell a narrow but painful workflow first:

- entity and wallet control
- transaction screening and case review
- audit logging and reporting
- AI-assisted human review

### Expand

Once the customer trusts TreasuryOS:

- add more internal teams
- add more entities and workflows
- add premium reporting
- add AI modules
- add integrations
- move toward deeper production posture over time

## Product packaging logic

Do **not** price TreasuryOS primarily like a commodity transaction API.

Price it around:

- operational control value
- compliance and review workload reduction
- governance and approval discipline
- audit and reporting readiness
- integration and rollout depth

## Recommended contract motion

### Motion 1 - Paid pilot

- scoped success criteria
- fixed onboarding and rollout plan
- limited initial operational surface
- explicit conversion decision point

### Motion 2 - Annual subscription

- convert after pilot proves value
- annual commitment preferred
- expansion through modules and workflow depth

### Motion 3 - Services and add-ons

- integrations
- reporting configuration
- workflow customization
- premium support

## The best business model for the next 12 months

If TreasuryOS wants the fastest credible path to revenue, the priority model is:

1. **sell paid pilots**
2. **convert to annual platform subscriptions**
3. **upsell implementation and premium modules**

That is the most realistic path from current product status to real business
traction.
