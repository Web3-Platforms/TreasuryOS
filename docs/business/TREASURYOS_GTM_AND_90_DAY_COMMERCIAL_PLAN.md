# TreasuryOS Go-To-Market and 90-Day Commercial Plan

## Goal

Turn TreasuryOS from a strong pilot-stage product into a real revenue-generating
business through founder-led enterprise motion.

## Recommended go-to-market model

### Primary motion

**Founder-led direct enterprise sales**

This is the right fit now because:

- the product is still early and high-context
- the problem is complex
- buyers need trust and education
- the first deals will likely be consultative, not transactional

### Secondary motion

**Design-partner and ecosystem relationship selling**

Use:

- investors and advisors for warm intros
- ecosystem operators for pilot candidates
- compliance, governance, and infrastructure partners for referral flow

## Core commercial story

TreasuryOS helps institutional teams run digital-asset treasury workflows with:

- governance-aware control
- compliance-aware review
- auditability and reporting
- AI-assisted human review

The first sale is not "buy our software because blockchain is cool."

The first sale is:

> "Use TreasuryOS to replace fragmented treasury/compliance workflow with one
> controlled operating layer."

## Best first customer motion

### Step 1 - Discovery

Target organizations that already have:

- treasury or digital-asset exposure
- internal approvals and review pain
- operational stakeholders beyond a single operator

### Step 2 - Paid pilot proposal

Sell a scoped pilot around:

- a painful existing workflow
- measurable review/control outcomes
- a defined timeline and success criteria

### Step 3 - Conversion

At the end of the pilot:

- convert to annual subscription
- expand into additional modules or integrations

## 90-day execution plan

### Days 1-15 - Commercial packaging

- finalize ICP and exclusion criteria
- finalize internal pricing bands
- prepare a standard paid-pilot offer
- prepare proposal, scope, and onboarding documents
- set up a simple CRM source of truth

### Days 16-30 - Pipeline creation

- build a target list of 25-50 accounts
- segment by best-fit category
- prepare founder outreach messages
- activate warm investor/advisor intros
- route every landing-page lead into a tracked sales workflow

### Days 31-60 - Sales conversations

- run discovery calls
- run product demos
- issue tailored paid-pilot proposals
- identify objections and update materials fast

### Days 61-90 - Pilot conversion

- close 1-2 paid pilots
- onboard them tightly
- collect usage, workflow pain, and objection data
- prepare one internal case-study draft from pilot outcomes

## Key commercial assets you should maintain

1. executive summary
2. investor / partner deck
3. paid-pilot one-pager
4. pricing and packaging sheet
5. proposal template
6. onboarding checklist
7. security and trust FAQ
8. product demo script

## Revenue targets for the near term

Do not optimize for vanity metrics first. Optimize for:

- the first paid pilot
- the first conversion to annual revenue
- the first repeatable ICP pattern

## Metrics that matter right now

| Metric | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Discovery calls booked | proves initial market pull |
| Paid pilots proposed | proves commercial qualification |
| Paid pilots closed | proves willingness to pay |
| Pilot-to-annual conversion | proves product and business viability |
| Time from intro to proposal | measures sales execution speed |
| Time from pilot start to conversion decision | measures commercialization efficiency |

## Recommended founder focus

Right now the founder should spend disproportionate time on:

1. customer discovery
2. demos
3. pilot packaging
4. objection handling
5. trust-building assets

Not on:

- broad paid acquisition
- self-serve funnels
- overbuilding features without customer pressure

## Best immediate business milestone

The strongest next business milestone is:

> **Close the first paid design partner and use that engagement to convert
> TreasuryOS from a pilot product into a referenceable business.**
