# TreasuryOS Business Status

## Short answer

TreasuryOS is now a **real pilot-stage business platform**, not just a codebase.
It has a live product surface, a credible institutional workflow, investor
material, a lead capture path, and a reusable AI-agent operating layer.

It is **not yet a fully mature regulated production business** because the trust
and commercial-operating layers are still incomplete.

## Current business posture

| Area | Status | What it means commercially |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Product core | Strong | There is a real platform to demo and pilot: dashboard, API, governance path, reporting, audit logging, case workflows |
| AI layer | Strong for pilot | AI advisory is live, controlled, and useful as a differentiator in demos |
| Market narrative | Strong | Investor docs, deck outline, landing page, and product story exist |
| Lead capture | Working | Landing page lead form is live and deploy path is fixed |
| Ops tooling | Improving | Agent operating system now exists for execution, documentation, and coordination |
| Revenue model | Designed, not locked | TreasuryOS can now package and price paid pilots, but pricing is still an internal working model |
| Trust layer | In progress | External audit is prepared but not yet engaged |
| Production integrations | In progress | KYC and banking production paths are not complete yet |
| On-chain production posture | Intentionally gated | Testnet-active is a credible pilot story, but not a full institutional production story |
| Customer proof | Weak so far | The next major business milestone is paid design partners and referenceable pilot wins |

## What makes TreasuryOS commercially credible today

### 1. There is a real product, not just a concept

TreasuryOS already has:

- a live dashboard
- a live API gateway
- entity and wallet workflows
- transaction-case review
- audit logging and reporting
- governance-aware architecture
- a real AI advisory slice

That is enough to run demos, pilot workflows, and paid early engagements.

### 2. The positioning is differentiated

TreasuryOS is not just a wallet tool, custody wrapper, or generic compliance
dashboard. It is positioned as the **control plane for institutional
digital-asset treasury**, combining:

- compliance workflow
- treasury operating workflow
- governance-aware execution design
- AI-assisted human review

That story is strong enough to sell into design-partner and early pilot motions.

### 3. The repo and docs now support repeatable execution

You now have:

- investor materials
- external audit preparation guides
- live product docs
- agent-role infrastructure for operations and growth work

This makes TreasuryOS much more executable as a business, not just as a product.

## What prevents TreasuryOS from being a fully mature business today

### Trust and assurance gaps

- external protocol audit not yet engaged or completed
- no audit remediation cycle closed yet
- no externally validated assurance story yet

### Production capability gaps

- Sumsub production rollout still deferred
- banking credentials and mTLS still pending
- mainnet posture intentionally gated

### Commercial operating gaps

- pricing and packaging were not yet formalized before this doc set
- CRM and sales workflow are not yet the visible source of truth
- no published customer proof, case study, or paid design-partner references yet
- billing, contracting, and customer onboarding playbooks still need to be
  standardized

## The honest business answer right now

### TreasuryOS is ready to sell

But it is ready to sell as:

- **paid design partner pilots**
- **institutional workflow pilots**
- **founder-led enterprise discovery and integration engagements**

It is **not** yet ready to sell as:

- broad self-serve SaaS
- fully regulated production infrastructure with every integration live
- a finished mainnet operating platform for every customer type

## Recommended commercial posture now

### Best current posture: paid pilot-first

The right near-term model is:

1. sell a paid pilot or design-partner engagement
2. convert successful pilots into annual software subscriptions
3. expand with add-ons, integrations, and premium service layers

This fits the actual state of TreasuryOS better than pretending the business is
already in wide-scale enterprise rollout mode.

## What you can sell immediately

### Offer 1 - Design Partner Pilot

Sell TreasuryOS as a structured pilot for institutional teams that need:

- workflow control
- wallet and transaction review
- auditability
- governance-aware operating discipline
- AI-assisted case analysis

### Offer 2 - Internal Treasury Operating Pilot

For teams already exploring digital-asset operations, TreasuryOS can be sold as
the internal operating layer for:

- policy-backed approvals
- case handling
- wallet governance
- reporting and evidence collection

### Offer 3 - Advisory + Implementation Engagement

For early customers, the product can be paired with onboarding and workflow
design support:

- control mapping
- workflow configuration
- reporting design
- rollout planning

## What TreasuryOS needs next to become a stronger business

1. close the first 1-2 paid pilots
2. stand up a simple CRM discipline and sales pipeline
3. create a standard offer, proposal, and onboarding flow
4. engage the external audit
5. finish production KYC and banking readiness
6. collect proof points from early customers

## Bottom-line status

TreasuryOS is now best described as:

> **A commercially credible pilot-stage institutional software business with a
> live product and clear monetization path, but not yet a fully mature
> production-scale regulated platform.**
