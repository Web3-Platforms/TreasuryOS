# AI Growth Recommendations

These recommendations assume TreasuryOS keeps AI as an **advisory product
surface**, not as a decision-maker.

## 1. Turn the first slice into a measurable operator win

Do not expand AI just because it is possible.

First measure whether transaction-case advisories reduce:

- review time
- repeat note-writing
- escalation friction
- incomplete evidence handoffs

If the first slice does not create a clear operator gain, do not scale it yet.

## 2. Build the feedback loop before a bigger provider bet

Before choosing a larger external model strategy, add product discipline around:

- accepted vs ignored advisories
- edited rationale frequency
- false-confidence complaints
- which checklist items operators actually use

That data will make the provider decision far stronger.

## 3. Expand by workflow, not by novelty

The best next AI surfaces are the ones that already have structured context:

1. wallet review context
2. report narrative drafts
3. audit-history summaries

Avoid broad chat-style AI inside the product until the narrow workflow surfaces
are proven.

## 4. Treat audit-prep AI as a go-to-market advantage

TreasuryOS can position AI as:

- an operator-speed layer
- an audit-readiness layer
- a documentation-quality layer

That is more credible than claiming autonomous compliance.

## 5. Keep the safety story productized

The safety boundaries are part of the product value:

- AI never signs
- AI never approves
- AI never bypasses RBAC
- AI stays reviewable and attributable

Institutional buyers will care about those boundaries as much as the model
itself.

## 6. Use the current slice to prepare for external assurance

A strong internal AI advisory layer can help TreasuryOS:

- organize evidence
- summarize controls
- speed up internal pre-audit review

But it should strengthen the future external audit story, not replace it.
