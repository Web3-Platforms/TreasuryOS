# TreasuryOS Pitch Deck Outline

Use this outline to build a clean investor deck without drifting away from the
current verified product posture.

## Slide 1 - Title

**Title:** TreasuryOS  
**Subtitle:** The compliance-first operating system for institutional
digital-asset treasury

**Talk track:** TreasuryOS gives institutions one platform to run digital-asset
treasury workflows with governance, auditability, and AI-assisted human review.

## Slide 2 - The problem

**Headline:** Institutional digital-asset operations are fragmented and hard to
control.

**Content:**

- treasury workflows are disconnected from compliance review
- wallet usage and governance are difficult to track in one place
- reporting and audit reconstruction are manual
- most stacks are not designed for institutional accountability

## Slide 3 - Why existing tools are not enough

**Headline:** Wallets, custody, compliance tools, and governance each solve only
part of the problem.

**Content:**

- custody is not the same as operational workflow
- multisig is not the same as policy-aware review
- blockchain access is not the same as institutional controls
- point solutions create operational gaps between teams

## Slide 4 - The solution

**Headline:** TreasuryOS unifies the institutional control layer.

**Content:**

- entity and wallet controls
- transaction screening and case handling
- governance-aware workflow design
- reporting and audit logging
- AI-assisted human review

## Slide 5 - Product today

**Headline:** The product is already live in pilot.

**Content:**

- Next.js dashboard and NestJS API
- live transaction-case workflow
- live reporting and audit logging
- manual Generate AI Analysis / Regenerate AI Analysis flow
- testnet-active Solana sync

**Proof note:** Use screenshots or product clips from the dashboard instead of
generic architecture art.

## Slide 6 - AI layer

**Headline:** AI is embedded as controlled review assistance.

**Content:**

- AI generates structured transaction-case analysis
- operator-triggered, not automatic
- metadata and fallback tracking are persisted
- no signer access
- no autonomous approvals

## Slide 7 - Why this matters to institutions

**Headline:** Institutions need an operating system, not just blockchain access.

**Content:**

- clearer accountability
- lower manual review overhead
- better operational consistency
- stronger audit posture
- easier bridge between traditional controls and digital-asset workflows

## Slide 8 - Why now

**Headline:** Digital-asset adoption needs regulated operational infrastructure.

**Content:**

- institutions need control before broader deployment
- governance and compliance expectations are rising
- AI can reduce review burden when embedded safely
- the category is moving from access tooling toward operating infrastructure

## Slide 9 - Go-to-market wedge

**Headline:** Start with the highest-friction institutional workflow.

**Content:**

- initial wedge: transaction screening and review operations
- buyer/user path: treasury, compliance, and operations teams
- expansion path: governance, reporting, wallet operations, and broader review surfaces

**Fill in before external use:**

- target customer profile
- pilot pipeline
- design partners
- sales motion

## Slide 10 - Business model

**Headline:** Enterprise infrastructure with workflow and control value.

**Suggested model framing:**

- platform subscription
- workflow or team-based pricing
- premium modules for AI-assisted review, integrations, and governance operations
- enterprise onboarding or integration services where appropriate

**Fill in before external use:**

- pricing model
- contract assumptions
- expected expansion motion

## Slide 11 - Roadmap

**Headline:** Trust expansion first, then broader production readiness.

**Content:**

1. close live AI canary and operator feedback loop
2. engage and complete external audit work
3. finish production KYC and banking integrations
4. expand AI to more read-only operational surfaces
5. move toward gated mainnet readiness after audit and governance approval

## Slide 12 - The ask

**Headline:** Back the control layer for institutional digital-asset treasury.

**Content:**

- capital to accelerate trust, integrations, and go-to-market
- strategic support with institutional introductions
- feedback from operators, compliance leaders, and treasury teams

**Fill in before external use:**

- fundraising amount
- round structure
- target investor profile
- specific use of funds

## Deck guardrails

- Do not insert invented traction metrics.
- Use placeholders until numbers are confirmed.
- Keep the main claim simple: TreasuryOS is already real, but trust expansion is
  the next milestone.
